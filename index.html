About Me

My name is Jiajie Li (李嘉杰), and I also go by Chance. I am a senior undergrad at School of Software Engineering, Tongji University, where I am fortunate to be jointly advised by Shengjie Zhao and Hongming Zhu. I am also pleased to have worked with Yan Zhang and Can Wang during my undergraduate years.

I am currently a visiting student at the City Science (formerly Changing Places) group at the MIT Media Lab. I previously interned at Microsoft Research Asia (MSRA) as a Research SDE and the Alan Turing Institute’s Urban analytics program as a research assistant. I am also a member of the City Science Network, and I am part of the City Science Lab@Shanghai. Refer to my resume for more information about my research experience

I have a wide range of interests, I am a national track and field athlete and a licensed TEDx organizer.

 

 

Research Interest

My research path and motivation for learning comes from my strong interest in R&D. My goal as a researcher is to use technology to make a positive impact on society nowadays, and I always try to explore the intersection of different disciplines and enjoy the creative process.

I have been programming since I was 11 years old and have been active in the open source community. Since starting my undergraduate studies, I have continued my computer science research, which has given me a wealth of experience in deep learning, computer vision, and mathematical modeling. But, as I’ve gotten deeper into the discipline, I’ve found that I’m not exactly at the center of the discipline, but rather enjoy exploring the connections between different disciplines, so I’ve been collaborating with colleagues from different disciplinary backgrounds.

In the last few years I have been focusing on city science research where I used various sensors to perceive the inhabitants and activities in the city. As my research became progressively deeper and more extensive, I realized that, despite tremendous efforts, protecting personal privacy in behavioral perception is still extremely challenging. So currently my research interests are focused on building a privacy-conscious data collection framework using bio-information (e.g. gait) as well as emerging sensors (e.g. neuromorphic vision sensors, event camera).
