Understanding Detailed Spatial Temporal Activities and Urban Vibrancy via Wi-Fi Big Data: Case of LivingLine Shanghai as an Urban Prototype

Abstract

Urban vibrancy is a topic of great concern in the field of urban design and planning. However, the definition and measurement of urban vibrancy have not been consistently and clearly followed. With the development of technologies such as big data and machine learning, urban researchers have been able to adopt some new methods that break the tradition to quantitatively evaluate urban vibrancy. This research attempts to quantify the fine-grained scale impact from the urban intervention of the LivingLine project, located in Siping Road Residential District, Shanghai through an empirical study. We use Wi-Fi probes to process the collected mobile phone data, and divide people into different groups according to commuting patterns; on this basis, we use a pre-trained random forest model to determine the specific locations of every single person. Subsequently, we analyzed the behavior patterns of people from tstay points detection and trajectory analysis. By building up statistical models, we utilize multi-linear regression and find that activity (urban intervention) and people’s behavior are positively correlated, from all three aspects mentioned above, which proves the impact of urban intervention on street dynamics. The research proposes a set of evidence-based workflows that can be generalized as a typical method for studying people behavior patterns in small-scale neighborhoods and streets without sacrificing users’ data privacy.
