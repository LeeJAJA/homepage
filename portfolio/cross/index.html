Cross-City Transfer Learning for Anti-epidemic Policies and Traffic Flow Prediction

Abstract

In recent years, with the maturity of sensing technology and computing environment, various kinds of big data have been quietly born in cities, such as traffic flow, weather data, road networks, points of interest, mobile trajectories and social media. However, the imbalance of urban development level and the heterogeneity of urban data make it extremely challenging to generalize urban computing on a large scale, and most of the existing urban knowledge migration work focuses on one specific task, modeling and optimizing for specific datasets. How to migrate urban knowledge learned from developed cities to data-sparse regions? How to generalize knowledge in urban spaces of different scales? How to unify similar semantic but heterogeneous data? To address these questions, this work proposes a general urban knowledge migration framework, which is based on representation learning to embed urban knowledge and uses an unsupervised adversarial learning method to map different urban embedding spaces so as to do knowledge migration. In this work, we performed migration learning experiments on four regions with different scales and degrees of development (Chengdu, Xi’an, London, and the island of Ireland) on two urban information tasks (predicting traffic flow using spatio-temporal models and controlling the spread of epidemics using reinforcement learning to assign road shares) and verified the effectiveness of the approach.

 

For more information on using reinforcement learning to assign road shares to control the spread of outbreaks, see the DURLECA Framework.
